Cookie Policy

Last Updated: October 21, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Grand Aura uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website. By using our website, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners. Cookies allow websites to remember your actions and preferences over time.

2. How We Use Cookies

Grand Aura uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Operations: Enable core functionality like user authentication and session management
  • Security: Protect against fraudulent activity and enhance platform security
  • Preferences: Remember your settings and preferences
  • Performance: Analyze how our website is used to improve user experience
  • Analytics: Understand visitor behavior and website traffic patterns

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Type Purpose Duration
Strictly Necessary Required for website functionality, authentication, and security Session or up to 1 year
Functional Remember your preferences and choices Up to 1 year
Performance Collect information about how you use our website Up to 2 years
Targeting/Advertising Deliver relevant advertisements (if applicable) Up to 2 years

4. Specific Cookies Used

4.1 Session Cookies

  • JSESSIONID: Maintains user session state
  • XSRF-TOKEN: Provides protection against cross-site request forgery attacks

4.2 Authentication Cookies

  • remember-me: Allows you to stay logged in across sessions
  • user-role: Stores your user role for access control

4.3 Preference Cookies

  • language: Remembers your language preference
  • theme: Stores your display preferences

5. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set their own cookies. These services may include:

  • Google Analytics: To analyze website traffic and user behavior
  • Payment Processors: To securely process payments
  • Social Media Plugins: To enable social sharing features

These third parties have their own privacy policies governing how they use your information.

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

6.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Delete all cookies from your browser
  • Block all cookies from being set
  • Allow only first-party cookies
  • Clear cookies when you close your browser

6.2 Browser-Specific Instructions

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data

6.3 Impact of Disabling Cookies

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may impact your experience on our website. Some features may not function properly, including:

  • User authentication and login sessions
  • Shopping cart and booking functionality
  • Preference settings
  • Personalized content

7. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want your online activities tracked. Currently, there is no industry-wide standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. Our website does not currently respond to DNT signals.

8. Mobile Applications

If we develop mobile applications in the future, they may use similar tracking technologies. You can control tracking through your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Tracking
  • Android: Settings → Privacy → Ads

9. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legislation, or our business practices. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when it was last revised.
